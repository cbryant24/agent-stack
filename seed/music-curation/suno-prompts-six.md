## Key Learnings from This Session

### 1. Balance is Everything in Nakano-Style Prompts
- **Too orchestral** = generic epic soundtrack (loses uniqueness)
- **Too electronic** = cold and mechanical (loses emotional core)
- **Sweet spot:** "Mechanical textures *blended with* warm strings" — fusion language is critical

### 2. Era-Specific References Matter
- "Vintage JRPG sound palette" immediately anchors to 90s-2000s game music
- "Nostalgic 90s game music aesthetic" helps Suno understand the sonic texture
- These references work better than vague "retro" descriptions

### 3. Emotional Descriptors Soften Mechanical Elements
- "Robotic atmosphere *softened by* emotional melodic themes"
- "Mechanical rhythms with melodic humanity"
- "Technological precision and organic warmth"
- These paired descriptors capture Nakano's signature style

### 4. Tempo Control Requires Multiple Descriptors
**For slow versions:**
- Explicit BPM (50-60 BPM)
- Pacing words: "glacial," "unhurried," "deliberate"
- Spaciousness: "space between phrases," "breathing room," "extended reverb"
- Quality words: "sustained," "patient," "reflective"

**For uptempo versions:**
- Explicit BPM (75-85 BPM)
- Energy words: "rhythmic," "driving," "propulsive," "forward momentum"
- Still maintain "contemplative" core

### 5. Lo-Fi vs. Pristine Slow
- "Lo-fi" adds tape saturation/degradation
- For clean slow versions: emphasize "sustained," "spacious," "pristine" without lo-fi references
- Both can be contemplative, but different sonic textures

### 6. Cosmic Lyrics Work Well with This Aesthetic
- Space/universe themes align naturally with Nakano's sci-fi RPG compositions
- Poetic, contemplative language fits the music style
- Both Japanese and French create atmospheric, cinematic feel
- Keep lyrics abstract and emotional rather than narrative-heavy

### 7. Character Limits Are Real But Manageable
- Suno's 1000-character limit is generous
- Our longest prompt: 683 characters
- Room for descriptive richness while staying under limit

## Best Practices Derived

### Building Nakano-Style Prompts:

1. **Start with genre fusion:** "Electronic orchestral RPG soundtrack"
2. **Add era anchor:** "Vintage JRPG sound palette," "90s game music aesthetic"
3. **Balance mechanical/organic:** Always pair cold descriptors with warm ones
4. **Include specific textures:** "Layered analog synthesizers," "warm string sections"
5. **Specify tempo if important:** Explicit BPM + descriptive pacing words
6. **Maintain emotional core:** "Contemplative," "introspective," "bittersweet"
7. **Add atmospheric depth:** "Ambient space atmosphere," "cosmic undertones"
8. **Tell a story:** "Story-driven composition," "cinematic storytelling"

### What to Avoid:

❌ Pure orchestral without electronic elements  
❌ Generic "epic" descriptors without specific textures  
❌ Cold mechanical descriptions without emotional balance  
❌ Vague "retro" without era-specific references  
❌ **NEVER use artist names** (translate qualities instead)
