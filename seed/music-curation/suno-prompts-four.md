## Iteration Learnings

### What Drives the Big-O Aesthetic
- The signature sound is **piano in dialogue with orchestra** — neither fully solo nor fully orchestral
- **Brass** (especially muted trumpet) is essential for the noir atmosphere
- **1950s film noir** as a reference point reliably pulls Suno toward that retro cinematic register
- **Anime soundtrack energy** is a useful descriptor when you want that emotionally volatile, sweeping quality

### Prompt Refinement Patterns
| Lever | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| `no drums, no bass, pure solo piano` | Strips arrangement to piano only — good for isolation, can feel sparse |
| `brooding brass stabs` | Adds drama and noir weight |
| `strings swelling softly beneath` | Keeps orchestra supportive without competing with piano |
| `tearful restraint` | Key phrase for emotional depth without melodrama |
| `grief and longing` vs `urgency` | Shifts emotional register from tense → sad |
| `long sustained notes dissolving into silence` | Gives Suno a pacing cue toward slow, deliberate delivery |

### Orchestral Balance Vocabulary
- **Piano dominant:** `solo piano as emotional centerpiece`, `piano as emotional anchor`
- **Balanced:** `dramatic solo piano lead`, `piano in dialogue with orchestra`
- **Orchestra dominant:** `jazz-inflected symphony`, `piano phrases dissolving into full orchestra`

### Mood Vocabulary That Worked
- `dark romanticism` — reliable noir/cinematic trigger
- `emotionally raw yet dignified` — prevents over-the-top melodrama
- `tearful restraint` — sadness with control
- `melancholic grandeur` — emotional weight with scale
- `muted trumpet in distance` — instant noir atmosphere signal

### General Suno Learnings
- **Specificity of instrument placement matters** — `muted trumpet in distance` generates differently than `muted trumpet`
- **Dynamic descriptors are pacing cues** — `sudden dynamic eruptions` vs `long sustained notes dissolving into silence` actively shapes Suno's output structure
- **Emotional vocabulary stacks** — layering `grief`, `longing`, `heartbroken`, `bittersweet` together reinforces the target mood more reliably than a single word
- **Era references work as style anchors** — `1950s noir` consistently pulls harmonic and production choices in the right direction
